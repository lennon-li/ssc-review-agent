# Reviewer Instructions — P.Stat.

The P.Stat. designation is awarded to applicants who meet the educational requirements of A.Stat. and have had six or more years of professional experience as a Statistician.

If the applicant is already an A.Stat., only the professional experience needs to be evaluated.

If the applicant is not an A.Stat. but has completed their university education relatively recently, say, within the past 12 years, they need to complete the checklist of courses, and their education will be evaluated as described in “Reviewer Instructions — A.Stat.”.

There will be applications from people who completed their formal education more than 12 years ago, possibly before university programs in Statistics were common, and, for them, the checklist of courses may not be relevant. They will need to show that they are widely knowledgeable in Statistics and expert in particular areas. This could be demonstrated by, for example, single-author papers on statistical methodology, a list of topics they have taught at an advanced university level or learned through professional development, or reports showing that they have applied appropriate statistical methods to non-standard problems.

The SSC does not currently offer accreditation in Data Science. An applicant whose training and professional experience have been primarily in Data Science may have insufficient statistical expertise to qualify for P.Stat. and it is up to the reviewers and the Accreditation Committee to decide.

Statistics graduates in other countries are eligible to apply for P.Stat. if they hold equivalent accreditation in their own country. We are beginning to receive such applications and find that they do not always meet our educational criteria. I have been insisting that they complete the checklist of courses and be evaluated on the same basis as a Canadian applicant. This is something for the Accreditation Committee to debate and make a recommendation.

Once you have assessed the educational requirements, read the letters of support and look for evidence of good communication. Look at their professional development, work experience, personal statements of how they meet the criteria for P.Stat., and any attached reports. Decide if they are keeping up to date, showing competence over a broad range of statistical problems, communicating their methods and results clearly, and showing a good understanding of professional ethics.

Please enter your report on the online form at https://form.jotform.com/SSCJotform/review-of-Pstat-application. When you sign and submit the form, it will go into a folder with an appropriate name in the folder “Review of P.Stat. Application” on Google Drive.

Reviewers do not communicate with the applicants. When the Accreditation Committee meets to review the reviewers’ reports, the Chair will prepare a recommendation for each applicant, to accept, reject or request more information. This report goes to the SSC Office, and they will inform the applicant.
