# Reviewer Instructions — A.Stat.

The A.Stat. designation is awarded to applicants who have completed a university-level program in Statistics but have had less than six years of professional experience. An A.Stat. applicant who has had six or more years of professional experience should be instructed to re-submit it as an application for P.Stat.

The checklist of courses defines what constitutes an acceptable program in statistics. If an applicant is missing a course, they can explain how they have otherwise acquired that skill, and the reviewer will have to assess that. If they have not filled out the checklist of courses, you might get a rough impression of the quality of the application and put that in your report but recommend that the applicant provide the checklist.

The reviewer should check that the courses correspond to the transcripts and should verify that each course is of the required standard. That is easy to do if they have studied at an institution with courses pre-approved through SSC Accreditation of Programs. If a course is not on our pre-approved list, they are asked to attach a course description and any other relevant information. Evaluating the courses is easier if they have studied at a well-known institution with a good reputation.

The “substantive area” courses should be courses in a subject-matter area where the application of statistical methodology plays an important role. Often an applicant will list a course there that isn’t quite appropriate, while their transcript include others that would be more suitable; in that case, you could recommend that the applicant provide descriptions of those courses, but the Accreditation Committee might decide that that is not necessary if the application is otherwise very strong.

The SSC does not currently offer accreditation in Data Science. A program in Data Science may have less emphasis on study design and classical statistical inference and might not satisfy the course checklist.

Statistics graduates in other countries are eligible to apply for A.Stat. if they hold equivalent accreditation in their own country. We are beginning to receive such applications and find that they do not always meet our educational criteria. I have been insisting that they complete the checklist of courses and be evaluated on the same basis as a Canadian applicant. This is something for the Accreditation Committee to debate and make a recommendation.

Once you have assessed the course checklist, all that remains is to read the letters of support and prepare your report.

Please enter your report on the online form at https://form.jotform.com/SSCJotform/review-of-Astat-application. When you sign and submit the form, it will go into a folder with an appropriate name in the folder “Review of A.Stat. Application” on Google Drive.

Reviewers do not communicate with the applicants. When the Accreditation Committee meets to review the reviewers’ reports, the Chair will prepare a recommendation for each applicant, to accept, reject or request more information. This report goes to the SSC Office, and they will inform the applicant.
